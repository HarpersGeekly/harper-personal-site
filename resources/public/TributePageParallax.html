Film Career and Notable Works

Ridley Scott's film career began in 1977 at the age of 40 when he directed his first motion picture, the historical drama The Duellists. The film received praise and recognition at the Cannes Film Festival winning Best First Work. His career work in the diverse genres of Science Fiction, Historical Epic, Crime Drama, and Comedy Drama are known for stunning visual atmosphere, often depicting adventurers in a foreign land adapting to challenging and hostile circumstances. It would be Scott's second feature film in 1979, Alien, that remains a testament to how a meticulous detail-oriented approach to filmmaking can elevate a standard story to first-class status and launch a successful ongoing career. He has been nominated four times for the Academy Award for Best Directing. His notable works include:

ALIEN (1979)

After a space merchant vessel perceives an unknown transmission as a distress call, its landing on the source moon finds one of the crew attacked by a mysterious lifeform, and they soon realize that its life cycle has merely begun.

BLADE RUNNER (1981)

A blade runner must pursue and terminate four replicants who stole a ship in space, and have returned to Earth to find their creator.

THELMA & LOUISE (1991)

Two best friends set out on an adventure, but it soon turns around to a terrifying escape from being hunted by the police, as these two girls escape for the crimes they committed.

GLADIATOR (2000)

When a Roman General is betrayed, and his family murdered by an emperor's corrupt son, he comes to Rome as a gladiator to seek revenge.

BLACK HAWK DOWN (2001)

160 elite U.S. soldiers drop into Somalia to capture two top lieutenants of a renegade warlord and find themselves in a desperate battle with a large force of heavily-armed Somalis.

AMERICAN GANGSTER (2007)

An outcast New York City cop is charged with bringing down Harlem drug lord Frank Lucas, whose real life inspired this partly biographical film.

PROMETHEUS (2012)

Following clues to the origin of mankind, a team finds a structure on a distant moon, but they soon realize they are not alone.

THE MARTIAN (2015)

An astronaut becomes stranded on Mars after his team assume him dead, and must rely on his ingenuity to find a way to signal to Earth that he is alive.
